Poverty is not created by the poor. It is created by the systems we have built, the institutions we have designed, and the policies we have put in place. Poor people are like bonsai trees. You take the seed of the tallest tree in the forest and plant it in a flowerpot. You get a replica of the tallest tree, but it is just inches tall. There is nothing wrong with the seed. It is the soil, the flowerpot, that is limiting the tree’s growth. In the same way, poor people are bonsai people. There is nothing wrong with their seed. Society simply never gave them the base to grow on.

Dr. Muhammad Yunus

Chief Adviser of the People's Republic of Bangladesh
